🎃 How Goblin Lines Work

Goblins move the line in your favor — easier to hit, lower multiplier. Key metric: played ÷ standard = delta%. A goblin at 90% prices very differently than one at 60%.

Example: Standard = 20.5. Goblin at 16.5 → delta% = 80.5%. Goblin at 12.0 → delta% = 58.5%.
Same 3-leg base of 6x, but PP prices the 58.5% goblin much lower.

Key insight: Log both lines every time — the label "Goblin" tells you nothing without knowing how far the line moved.
😈 How Demon Lines Work

Demons move the line against you — harder to hit, higher multiplier. Delta% will be above 1.0. A demon at 110% is shallow; one at 140%+ is extreme.

Example: Standard = 24.5. Demon at 26.5 → delta% = 108%. Demon at 34.5 → delta% = 141%.
PP captures extra margin on Demon lines — log observations to see if the boost is actually worth it.

Key insight: Demon boosts are often not proportional to actual difficulty.
🧮 Mixed Slip Payout Logic
PrizePicks does not publish the formula. Based on observations:

1. Base multiplier = standard payout for leg count (e.g. 6x for 3 legs)
2. Each Goblin/Demon applies a factor based on its delta%, not just its label
3. Goblin factor reduces payout; Demon factor increases it
4. Factors multiply together — Goblin + Demon partially offset each other

The exact per-leg factor is dynamic and undisclosed. Logging real observations is the only way to reverse-engineer the actual curve.
